Host an AirNode

Host an AirNode to bring connectivity to your community, earn rewards, and grow the World Mobile network —no equipment or technical expertise required.

What is an AirNode?

An AirNode is a wireless access point that connects local users to World Mobile's network, delivering internet, voice calls, and text messaging services to your community.

What does a Host do?

An AirNode Host provides a strategic location for World Mobile's network equipment and earns rewards based on the connectivity services delivered through their hosted AirNode.

What is required?

You don't need to purchase any equipment—World Mobile's AirNode operators handle that. You just need:

  • A suitable location
  • Reliable power supply for the AirNode
  • Ability to provide maintenance access when needed

AirNode Host: How Much Can You Earn?

By hosting an AirNode, you receive a share of the rewards generated from providing connectivity to your community. Your earnings grow as more users connect through your hosted AirNode. Potential earnings with our more powerful AirNodes:

Frequently Asked Questions

What Are the Requirements to Become an AirNode Host with World Mobile?

To become an AirNode Host, you need access to a rooftop in an eligible location, a reliable power supply, and a business-grade internet connection. You may also need to provide maintenance access to the AirNode if required.

Can You Explain the Maintenance Requirements and Schedule?

AirNodes are low-maintenance devices. They require a stable power supply and internet connection. Regular maintenance is unnecessary unless there is damage caused by external factors.

Does the Usage Data Remain Private?

Yes, World Mobile prioritizes data privacy. Operators can only view traffic volume on their dashboard. Your data is encrypted and cannot be accessed or decrypted by operators.

Will Hosting an AirNode Slow Down My Home Internet?

Any slowdown will be minimal and depends on your internet speed. With a robust connection, hosting an AirNode should not noticeably impact your internet performance.
